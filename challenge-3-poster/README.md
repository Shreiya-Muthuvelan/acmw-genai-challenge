## 💡 Concept: The Memory Shop

> *In the future, you can buy and sell memories.*

**Memory Shop** is a fictional storefront set in a near-future world where human memories have become commodities — precious, tradeable, and archivable. The poster imagines the shopfront of this establishment: a place where people come to deposit forgotten childhoods, purchase someone else's joy, or preserve emotions before they fade.

The concept is built around three distinct pillars, each representing a service the shop offers:

| Pillar | Tagline | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 📼 Chronicle of Lost Times | *Recording Yesterday* | A service for capturing and archiving personal memories |
| 💎 Precious Things Collector | *We Buy and Sell Memories* | The transactional core of the shop — memory as currency |
| 🏛️ Sanctuary of Stories | *Preserved Emotions and Forgotten Knowledge* | A vault for memories too important to let fade |

## 🎨 Mood & Visual Direction

The poster aesthetic leans into **nostalgic surrealism** — evoking the feeling of a half-remembered dream. The visual language mixes antique shop warmth with a slightly eerie, otherworldly quality, reflecting the uncanny nature of a world where memory is for sale.

**Mood keywords:** Nostalgic · Cinematic · Warm-toned · Slightly haunting · Intimate

---

## 🖼️ Initial Concepts

Three individual visuals were generated using AI, each exploring a different visual interpretation of the Memory Shop concept. These served as raw material — not finished outputs — and were later combined and refined into the final poster.

| Concept | Focus |
|---|---|
| Concept 1 | Chronicle of Lost Times — archival, tape-reel aesthetic |
| Concept 2 | Precious Things Collector — shop window, warm amber tones |
| Concept 3 | Sanctuary of Stories — vault-like, ethereal lighting |

> 📌 See `concepts/` folder for all three images.

---

## 🛠️ Refinement Process

The three AI-generated concept images were composited and refined into a single cohesive poster. The process involved:

1. **AI Generation** — Individual visuals created via the notebook
2. **Composition** — The three concept images were arranged and layered into a unified poster layout
3. **Typography** — The three pillar texts (*Chronicle of Lost Times*, *Precious Things Collector*, *Sanctuary of Stories*) and their taglines were overlaid with careful font and placement choices
4. **Color Grading** — A unified warm, slightly desaturated tone was applied across all elements to make the combined poster feel cohesive rather than patchwork
5. **Final Polish** — Contrast, balance, and spacing were adjusted for a print-ready feel

## 📝 Design Rationale

The core design challenge was making three independently generated images feel like they belonged together. Rather than forcing a uniform style at the generation stage, we leaned into the variation and used post-processing and typography to unify them. The three-panel structure mirrors the three services of the shop, making the layout itself part of the storytelling. The text choices — poetic and slightly archaic in tone — were deliberate: they feel like they belong in the window of an old curiosity shop, which is exactly the atmosphere the concept calls for.
